Smart TV Privacy Guide

Step-by-step instructions for Samsung, LG, Sony, and Vizio TVs

What Data Does Your Smart TV Collect?

Smart TVs collect viewing history, search terms, app usage, and use ACR (Automatic Content Recognition) technology to track everything you watch.

Privacy Settings by Brand

Samsung Smart TV Privacy Settings

1 Disable Voice Recognition

  1. Press Home button on remote
  2. Go to Settings (gear icon)
  3. Select "General" → "Voice"
  4. Turn off "Voice Recognition"

2 Turn Off ACR (Viewing Information Services)

  1. Go to Settings → "Support"
  2. Select "Terms & Privacy"
  3. Choose "Viewing Information Services"
  4. Toggle OFF

3 Disable Interest-Based Ads

  1. Settings → "Support" → "Terms & Privacy"
  2. Select "Interest-Based Advertising"
  3. Choose "Opt Out"

LG Smart TV Privacy Settings

1 Disable Voice Recognition

  1. Press Settings button on remote
  2. Go to "General" tab
  3. Select "AI Service"
  4. Turn off "AI Recommendation" and "Voice Recognition"

2 Turn Off Live Plus (ACR)

  1. Settings → "General"
  2. Select "Live Plus"
  3. Toggle OFF

3 Disable Ad Tracking

  1. Settings → "General" → "Additional Settings"
  2. Select "Privacy" → "Ad Tracking"
  3. Toggle OFF

Sony (Google TV) Privacy Settings

1 Disable ACR (Samba Interactive TV)

  1. Press Home button
  2. Go to Settings → "Device Preferences"
  3. Select "Samba Interactive TV"
  4. Toggle OFF

2 Google Assistant Settings

  1. Settings → "Device Preferences" → "Google Assistant"
  2. Turn off "Voice Match"
  3. Disable "Personal Results"

Vizio Smart TV Privacy Settings

1 Disable Smart Interactivity (ACR)

  1. Press Menu button on remote
  2. Go to "System" → "Reset & Admin"
  3. Select "Smart Interactivity"
  4. Choose "Off"

2 Turn Off Viewing Data

  1. Same menu path as above
  2. Select "Viewing Data"
  3. Toggle OFF

Universal Privacy Tips

General Privacy Protection

  • Use an external streaming device (Roku, Apple TV, Chromecast) - they often have better privacy controls
  • Disconnect your TV from the internet if you don't need smart features
  • Cover built-in cameras with tape or a camera cover
  • Update firmware regularly for security patches
  • Review privacy settings monthly as updates may reset them
