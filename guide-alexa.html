Amazon Alexa Privacy Guide

Complete guide to securing your Alexa device and protecting your voice data

What Data Does Alexa Collect?

Amazon Alexa collects various types of data to provide its services. Understanding what's collected is the first step to protecting your privacy.

Voice Recordings

Your commands and interactions

Location Data

Your address and time zone

Contact Lists

If you sync your contacts

Purchase History

Items bought through Alexa

Step-by-Step Privacy Settings

1 Review and Delete Voice History

  1. Open the Alexa app on your smartphone
  2. Tap on "More" (three lines) at the bottom right
  3. Select "Settings" → "Alexa Privacy"
  4. Tap on "Review Voice History"
  5. You can:
    • Listen to individual recordings
    • Delete recordings by date range
    • Delete all recordings at once

2 Disable Human Review of Recordings

  1. Go to "Settings" → "Alexa Privacy"
  2. Tap on "Manage Your Alexa Data"
  3. Find "Use Voice Recordings to Improve Amazon Services"
  4. Toggle this setting OFF
  5. Confirm your choice when prompted
Tip: This prevents Amazon employees from listening to your recordings for quality improvement purposes.

3 Set Up Automatic Deletion

  1. In "Alexa Privacy" settings
  2. Tap on "Manage Your Alexa Data"
  3. Under "Voice Recordings"
  4. Select "Automatically delete recordings"
  5. Choose your preferred timeframe:
    • 3 months
    • 6 months
    • 18 months

4 Manage Skills and Permissions

  1. Go to "More" → "Skills & Games"
  2. Tap on "Your Skills"
  3. Review each skill's permissions
  4. Disable or remove skills you don't use
  5. Check what data each skill can access

Important Security Settings

  • Enable Voice Code: Set up a voice code for purchases and sensitive information
  • Turn off "Alexa Drop In": Disable if you don't want others to drop in unannounced
  • Review Communication Permissions: Check who can call or message your Alexa
