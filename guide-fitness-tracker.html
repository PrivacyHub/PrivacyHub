Fitness Tracker Privacy Guide

Secure your health data, manage app permissions, and disable public sharing

What Data Do Fitness Trackers Collect?

Fitness trackers collect highly sensitive personal and health data. Understanding what's being collected is crucial for protecting your privacy.

Heart Rate

Continuous monitoring, resting rate, workout peaks

Sleep Patterns

Duration, quality, REM cycles

GPS Location

Running routes, frequently visited places

Step Count

Daily activity levels

Calories Burned

Energy expenditure

Payment Info

If using contactless payments

Why This Data Is Sensitive

Health data can reveal medical conditions, daily routines, and location history. This information could affect insurance rates, employment opportunities, and personal privacy if compromised.

Privacy Settings by Device

Fitbit Privacy Settings

1 Disable Public Sharing

  1. Open Fitbit app on your smartphone
  2. Tap your profile picture (top left)
  3. Select "Settings" → "Privacy"
  4. Under "Personal Info Sharing":
    • Turn off "Share my activity stats"
    • Disable "Share my sleep stats"
    • Turn off "Share my weight stats"
  5. Set "Profile Visibility" to "Private" or "Friends Only"

2 Manage App Permissions

  1. In Fitbit app, go to "Settings"
  2. Tap "Connected Apps"
  3. Review all connected third-party apps
  4. Remove any apps you don't use by tapping "Revoke Access"

3 Disable Ad Personalization

  1. Go to "Settings" → "Privacy"
  2. Find "Ad Personalization"
  3. Toggle OFF

4 Secure Your Account

  1. Enable two-factor authentication in account settings
  2. Use a strong, unique password
  3. Regularly review login history

Garmin Privacy Settings

1 Control Profile Visibility

  1. Go to Garmin Connect app
  2. Tap "More" (bottom right)
  3. Select "Settings" → "Profile Visibility"
  4. Set to "Private" or "Connections Only"

2 Manage Activity Sharing

  1. In Garmin Connect, go to "Settings"
  2. Select "Activity Privacy"
  3. Choose default privacy for all activities:
    • "Only Me" - Most private
    • "Connections" - Share with friends only
    • "Public" - Avoid this option

3 Review Connected Apps

  1. Go to "Settings" → "Connected Apps"
  2. Review list of apps with access
  3. Remove any unfamiliar or unused apps
Tip: Garmin offers "LiveTrack" which shares your real-time location during activities. Disable this if not needed.

Apple Watch Privacy Settings

1 Control Health App Permissions

  1. Open the Health app on iPhone
  2. Tap your profile picture (top right)
  3. Select "Privacy" → "Apps"
  4. Review each app's access to health data
  5. Toggle OFF access for any unnecessary apps

2 Manage Device Permissions

  1. Open Watch app on iPhone
  2. Go to "Privacy"
  3. Review and disable:
    • Heart Rate
    • Fitness Tracking
    • Noise Monitoring
    • Location Services

3 Disable Share Health Data

  1. In Health app, go to "Sharing"
  2. Review who you're sharing with
  3. Stop sharing with anyone unnecessary
  4. Turn off "Share With Your Doctor" if not needed

Apple Watch Series Features

Newer Apple Watches include features like fall detection, ECG, and blood oxygen monitoring. Review these settings and disable any you don't need.

Samsung Galaxy Watch Privacy Settings

1 Control Samsung Health Privacy

  1. Open Samsung Health app
  2. Tap the three lines (menu)
  3. Select "Settings" → "Privacy"
  4. Turn off "Share activity data"
  5. Disable "Marketing notifications"

2 Manage App Permissions

  1. Go to Galaxy Wearable app
  2. Tap "Watch settings" → "Apps"
  3. Review app permissions
  4. Remove access for unused apps

3 Disable Samsung Cloud Sync

  1. In Samsung Health settings
  2. Find "Sync with Samsung Cloud"
  3. Toggle OFF if you prefer local storage

Universal Privacy Tips

Account Security

  • Use strong, unique passwords for each fitness app
  • Enable two-factor authentication everywhere possible
  • Regularly review login history for suspicious activity

App Permissions

  • Review app permissions monthly - apps often request more access than needed
  • Disable location tracking when not needed for specific activities
  • Turn off public sharing features - your workouts don't need to be public
  • Be careful with social media connections - avoid auto-posting workouts

Data Management

  • Regularly delete old activity data you no longer need
  • Download your data before deleting accounts
  • Factory reset your device before selling or giving away
  • Remove device from your account when replacing it
