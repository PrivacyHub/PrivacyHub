Smart Doorbell Cameras: Security or Privacy Risk?

November 30, 2025 6 min read By Asayel Hasan

What Data Do They Collect?

Smart doorbells record video footage, audio conversations, motion alerts, timestamps, and sometimes facial recognition data. Recordings are usually stored in cloud servers, accessible from your smartphone. These devices are always ready to capture activity near your door, creating a comprehensive log of everyone who approaches your home.

How Do They Track Activity?

These devices monitor movement near your door using motion sensors. When motion is detected, they begin recording automatically and send alerts to your smartphone. Advanced models use person detection, package detection, and even facial recognition to differentiate between familiar faces and strangers. Some can track motion patterns and predict when deliveries typically occur.

What Are the Risks?

Stored footage may be shared with third-party companies or, in some cases, law enforcement without your knowledge. If accounts are not secure, hackers could access private recordings and live feeds. Neighbors and visitors may be recorded without consent, raising ethical and legal questions. Some doorbell companies have faced criticism for allowing employees to access customer videos. Facial recognition data could be misused or breached.

Types of Data Collected:

  • Video Footage: Continuous or motion-activated recordings of your property and visitors
  • Audio Recordings: Conversations captured near your door
  • Motion Data: Timestamps and patterns of detected movement
  • Facial Recognition: If enabled, data about familiar faces
  • Person Detection: Whether motion was caused by a person, vehicle, or animal
  • Package Detection: Delivery notifications and package presence
  • Geofencing Data: Your location to arm/disarm alerts
  • Device Information: Signal strength, battery status, firmware version

How to Stay Safe

  1. Use strong passwords: Create a unique, complex password for your doorbell account. Never reuse passwords.
  2. Enable two-factor authentication: Add an extra layer of security to prevent unauthorized access.
  3. Adjust motion sensitivity: Reduce false alerts and unnecessary recordings by fine-tuning motion zones and sensitivity.
  4. Limit access: Only grant account access to trusted family members, and remove access when no longer needed.
  5. Regularly review and delete footage: Clear old recordings that are no longer needed. Check storage settings.
  6. Update firmware: Keep the doorbell's software updated for the latest security patches.
  7. Understand privacy zones: Set up privacy zones to block recording of neighbors' property or public sidewalks.
  8. Review cloud storage policies: Understand where your footage is stored and for how long. Consider local storage options if available.

Legal Considerations

In many jurisdictions, recording audio without consent may be illegal. Check local laws about recording conversations in public or shared spaces. Some areas have specific regulations about cameras pointing at public property or neighbors' homes. Consider posting notices if your doorbell camera records public areas.

Police Requests for Footage

Some doorbell companies have partnerships with law enforcement, making it easier for police to request footage without a warrant. Research your device's policies on sharing data with authorities. In some cases, you can opt out of these programs or require a warrant for access.
