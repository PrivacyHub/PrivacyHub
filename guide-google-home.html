Google Home Privacy Guide

Complete guide to securing your Google Assistant and protecting your data

What Data Does Google Home Collect?

Google Home devices collect various types of data through your interactions with Google Assistant.

Voice Commands

All interactions with Google Assistant

Location History

Your home and work addresses

Calendar Events

If you sync your calendar

Watch History

On devices with screens

Step-by-Step Privacy Settings

1 Review and Delete Google Assistant Activity

  1. Open the Google Home app on your smartphone
  2. Tap on your profile picture (top right)
  3. Select "Google Assistant settings"
  4. Tap on "Your data in the Assistant"
  5. Select "Assistant activity" to view all recordings
  6. To delete:
    • Tap the "X" next to individual items
    • Use "Delete activity by" to delete by date range
    • Choose "Delete all time" to clear everything

2 Set Up Auto-Delete for Activity

  1. Go to "Your data in the Assistant"
  2. Tap on "Activity controls"
  3. Find "Web & App Activity"
  4. Tap on "Auto-delete"
  5. Choose your preference:
    • Auto-delete after 3 months
    • Auto-delete after 18 months
    • Auto-delete after 36 months
  6. Confirm your selection
Tip: Setting auto-delete to 3 months balances functionality with privacy.

3 Disable Personalized Ads

  1. Visit myactivity.google.com on your browser
  2. Go to "Activity controls"
  3. Find "Ad personalization"
  4. Toggle it OFF
  5. Confirm your choice
This prevents Google from using your activity to personalize ads across Google services.

4 Manage Voice Match and Recognition

  1. In Google Home app, tap your device
  2. Tap Settings (gear icon)
  3. Select "Recognition & sharing"
  4. Review "Voice Match" settings
  5. You can:
    • Remove voice models
    • Disable personalized results
    • Turn off "Hey Google" sensitivity
